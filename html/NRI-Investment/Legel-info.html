Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) are recognized under the Foreign Exchange Regulatory Act, 1973. As per RBI guidelines, banks and housing finance companies define an NRI as “an Indian citizen who holds a valid Indian passport and resides abroad for employment, business, or any vocation, or stays overseas under circumstances indicating an intention for an indefinite period.” At Vanik Ventures, we understand the specific needs of NRIs and offer tailored real estate investment solutions to help them invest seamlessly in their homeland. 4o

Legal Information

(Law and Procedure)

Below, we have compiled essential legal information to assist you in purchasing a home or property in India.

  • Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) holding Indian passports do not require prior permission from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) to acquire immovable property for bona fide residential purposes in India.
  • Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) holding Indian passports can pay the purchase consideration either by remitting funds from abroad through normal banking channels or using their NRO, NRE, or FCNR account.
  • Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) are advised to obtain a copy of the title report from a solicitor to ensure the property's legal clarity. It is essential to verify that there are no hidden conditions in the fine print and no specific reservations imposed by the state government.
  • Ensure that the property has all necessary clearance reports. For example, if the construction is near a seafront, check for Coastal Regulation Zone (CRZ) clearance. If the project is close to a heritage site, verify any heritage reservations that may apply. The goal is to avoid legal complications or potential disputes. Additionally, unresolved title issues may prevent you from securing a home loan. At Vanik Ventures, we prioritize transparency and due diligence, making NRI property investment in India smooth, secure, and hassle-free.
